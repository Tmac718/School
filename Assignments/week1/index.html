Marble

Photo by Henry & Co

Marble is a metamorphic stone, meaning it transforms from one rock type(Limestone) to another. All marble has a one-of-a-kind pattern. Yes, like a zebra!

There is a reason so many buildings use it. In addition to its beauty, it is very durable. The material does not retain heat and it is hypoallergenic.

Geographic locations for Marble was explained best by Natural Stone Online. They state "Marble quarries occur across Turkey, Greece and the US, but the most famous and treasured source is southern Italy".

Photo by Denise Duplinski

Granite

Photo by Bareth David

Granite is both an igneous rock and a plutonic rock, meaning it is made of cooled lava forming deep underground. It is one of the hardest substances on earth, second only to diamonds!

Granite is one of the oldest known rocks, being formed as long as 300 million years ago. Amazing fun fact, it was the reason behind the first commercial railroad in the United States.

Geographic locations for granite as pointed out by Granitemarblewa are "Traditionally, Italy, USA, and Canada are very popular sources of granite, but China, India, and several locations in Africa and South America are big suppliers of granite to the rest of the world."

Photo by May Guo

Limestone

Photo by Photoholgic

Limestone is a sedimentary rock. being formed by sediment like shells, silt, and sand settling at the bottom of lakes and oceans the layers of sediment can be seen in the stone similar to rings in a tree.

Finding fossils in limestone is common you know the pointy things that hang in caves, they are called stalagmites and stalactites and are made up entirely of limestone!

Geographic locations for limestone WorldAtlas explains are "China, the US, Russia, Japan, India, Brazil, Germany, Mexico, and Italy are some of the world's largest limestone prouducers today.

Some of the biggest quarries in the world, however, are in the U.S. state of Michigan, specifically near the Great Lakes' coastlines."
